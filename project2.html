Starfield

Background

This term, I read a book called Physics of the Future, written by Michio Kaku. His description of the space is very attractive to me so for this project I want to try if I can depict the starfield by code.

Controllers

For this project, I used those controllers:

Slide Potentiometers

Rotary Potentiometers

Button

How controllers affect my code

I designed the way the controllers interact with my code according to their operational characteristics.

Slide Potentiometer Changes The Speed

For slide potentiometers, you can slide up or down, so it fits for controlling the speed of the stars.

Rotary Potentiometer Changes The Rotation

For the rotary potentiometers, just like its name, you can rotate them to adjust the data. So I use that to mimic the rotation of the starfield.

Button Changes Display Mode

For the button, I used that to change the mode to turn on and turn off the effect.

Design the physical shape of controllers

I set up the physcial shape of my controllers by using a paper box I had at home. I dug three holes in the cardboard, where the slide potentiometer and rotary potentiometer I attached directly to the surface of the box and connected to the photon board with male and female wires.

For the button, since it is too small to stick on the surface of the box, I cut a piece of wood stick with a saw and set it on top of the button, leaving the top of the stick exposed outside the carton. When pressed, the stick presses down on the button to complete the interaction.
